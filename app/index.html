My journey!

I’ve practised yoga for over 20 years. During this time there have been occassions when I didn’t practice yoga, when life just took over, but something always brought me back to a yoga class. When I didn’t do yoga, I just didn’t feel good - I simply wasn’t the best me.

The love of yoga has changed my life and I am so grateful for that. I sat at a desk in the corporate world for 20 years and always knew something was missing.

When I realised that yoga was so much more than just the physical practice I wanted to explore more of its wonderful benefits. This curiosity drove me to embark on a 500 hour teacher training throught the British Wheel of Yoga with the very experienced, and inspirational, Rosemary Bennett as my teacher trainer. I was hooked and my journey of becoming a yogi began.

The two years training opened my eyes as I learnt about yoga philosophy, meditation and relaxation, anatomy and physiology, about teaching safely and a whole lot more about myself.

It was amazing, and I met some wonderful and talented fellow yogis who are now out there teaching as well.

I couldn't imagine this change in my life when I worked in an office, but true to my learnings - I never look back, I look forward to my tomorrows but always rejoice in today.

You & the benefits of Yoga

For thousands of years the benefits of yoga have been well documented, and the same poses and breathing exercises have stood the test of time - it’s never been a fad, it’s about truth. Though my own personal experiences, the physical, mental and spiritual practices have helped me through some challenging times; it has uplifted me, re energised me and taught me how to focus my mind.

When you invite yoga into your life you will:

✔   strengthen your body

✔   improve your flexibility

✔   let go of held tension throughout the body

✔   focus on easing and freeing the breath

✔   learn to quieten the ever chattering thoughts that disturb the mind allowing you to focus

✔   achieve a sense of balance both physically and mentally

✔   switch off from the outside world and be in the moment

✔   learn to listen to your mind and body and accept its teachings

✔   have more energy, sleep better and return to your natural state of health and well-being

But its so much more than the physical and mental benefits, its so muh more than movement and breath. the greatest goal is to learn about yourself and take what fills your heart and mind into your life every day to enrivh your world. It brings peace.

Yoga is a journey and if you let it, it can be the most wonderful journey you will ever experience. You may not ever get into some of the yoga poses, but exploring your body and mind is amazing.

Class information

What to expect in a class

Centring

Our Hatha class will start with a short centring exercise, allowing you the space to prepare for your time on the mat/chair.

Asana

We will then start warming up the body and mind and then move into asana practice (posture practice) which can include standing and seated poses, twists, side bends, forward bends, balances and inversions.

Pranayama

During classes we will also explore the breath and practice specific breathing techniques (pranayama).

Relaxation and Meditation

Each class ends with 10-15 minutes of relaxation and/or meditation.

All postures can be adapted for people at different levels or with certain medical conditions.

Other information

Before a class

Try not to eat a big meal about 2 hours before a class. A light meal or snack is okay if you need something to eat.

What to bring

For the mat based classes all you need to bring is your own mat or if you havent got a mat just yet, a towel will do.

Water is available at the venue but you may wish to bring your own water bottle in case you get thirsty..

I will provide other props that we may need such as blocks and belts, but if you have your own feel free to bring them.

Other things to know

We practice yoga in bare feet but please if your shy about your feet, do not let this put you off and its good to know that there are special sticky yoga socks you can buy if you would really would prefer not to practice with bare feet.
